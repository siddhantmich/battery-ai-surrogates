Project Summary

Rapid assessment and accurate forecasting of the battery state- of-health are essential for Lithium-ion battery usage and lifespan optimization. However, physics-based solvers are computationally intensive, and traditional surrogate models fail to handle time-series data and dynamically changing conditions. In this paper, the authors investigate a traditional deep learning model, LSTM, for its utility as a good surrogate model for the computationally expensive physics solvers, as well as the neural operators DeepONet and a state-space model modification called Mamba-DeepONet, for surrogate mod- eling of Single Particle Model simulations. Our results show that while DeepONet is not very good at capturing the battery dynamics, both LSTM and Mamba-DeepONet can function as effective sur- rogate models for batteries. We then conduct parameter inference using Tree-structured Parzen Estimator (TPE) algorithm and find that both LSTM and Mamba-DeepONet models show similar perfor- mance for parameter inference. Owing to the high parallelizability and the potential to train on much longer time-series sequences in the future, we make a case for why Mamba-DeepONet should be used for surrogate battery models in the future.
